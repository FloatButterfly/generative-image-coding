Abstract

As generative technologies advance, visual content has evolved into a complex mix of natural and AI-generated images, driving the need for more efficient coding techniques that prioritize perceptual quality. Traditional codecs and learned methods struggle to maintain subjective quality at high compression ratios, while existing generative approaches face challenges in visual fidelity and generalization. We propose a generative coding framework leveraging diffusion priors to enhance compression performance at low bitrates. Our approach employs a pre-optimized encoder to generate generalized compressed-domain representations, integrated with pretrained modelâ€™s internal features via a lightweight adapter and attentive fusion module. A distribution renormalization method further enhances reconstruction fidelity. Extensive experiments show our method (1) outperforms existing methods in visual fidelity across low bitrates, (2) improves compression performance by up to 79% over H.266/VVC, and (3) offers an efficient solution for AI-generated content while being adaptable to broader content types.

Selected Results

Compression Performance

Our approach significantly reduces bitrate while maintaining perceptual quality. Compared with VVC and learned codecs such as Cheng-VBR and TCM-VBR, our method achieves up to 79% bitrate savings while delivering sharper textures and improved structure preservation.

Generalizability

The framework generalizes well to both AI-generated and natural image domains. With minimal adapter finetuning, it achieves superior results across datasets including DiffusionDB and Kodak, demonstrating strong adaptability to diverse content distributions.

R-D Curves on AIGC and Kodak datasets. Our method achieves consistent bitrate savings and perceptual fidelity improvements over state-of-the-art baselines across diverse image types.
